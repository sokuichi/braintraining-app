Evidence shelf

Short version: creativity is trainable, especially when drills target cognitive skills and problem-solving processes.

  • Scott, Leritz, and Mumford meta-analysis Creativity training showed strongest gains for divergent thinking and problem solving.
  • Sio and Ormerod incubation meta-analysis Incubation breaks can help, especially after preparation and for divergent tasks.
  • Colzato, Ozturk, and Hommel meditation study Open-monitoring meditation was associated with better divergent thinking performance.
  • Baas, De Dreu, and Nijstad mood-creativity meta-analysis Positive mood can support creativity, with effects depending on task and intensity.
